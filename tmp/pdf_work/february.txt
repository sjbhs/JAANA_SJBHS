About The OBA Calling
The OBA Calling is more than a newsletter. It is a structured communication initiative
designed to strengthen the bond between SJBHS OBA and its members across the globe.

At its core, the OBA Calling seeks to inform, inspire, and involve. It documents the activities,
initiatives, and outreach efforts undertaken by the Old Boys’ Association.

Presenting The OBA Calling Trilogy

The OBA Calling February 2026 has been envisioned as a three-part series, each dedicated to
a vital dimension of the Association’s mission.

Part One: Milestones and Engagements.

This edition captures the vibrancy of the OBA calendar, including tournaments, connects,
outreach initiatives, fellowship gatherings, flagship programes, and institutional collaborations.
It reflects the scale, energy, and unity that define our community.

Part Two: Giving with Purpose

The second edition will focus on donations, outlining the causes supported by the OBA,
contributions received and their impact, mechanisms to donate and the governance
processes that ensure accountability. It aims to provide clarity, transparency, and confidence
to all who wish to give back.

Part Three: Spotlight on Josephites

The final edition will celebrate individual Old Boys, their achievements, leadership journeys,
contributions to society, and continued association with the School. It will highlight stories that
inspire and reinforce the legacy of excellence associated with St. Joseph’s Boys High School.

Through this trilogy, OBA Calling aspires to move beyond event reporting. It seeks to build
awareness, encourage participation, deepen engagement and cultivate a culture of sustained
contribution.

Message from The Principal
Dear Old Boys and Girls,

In the words of John F Kennedy: “I think the success of any
school can be measured by the contribution the alumni make
to our national life”. This quote comes alive when we look at the
contribution made by our alumni across the globe. However,
what stirs our hearts even more is the undeniable fact that our
alumni always remember their alma mater with a love so deep
and a yearning to give back that is incomparable!
My heart swells with gratitude to each of you for your steadfast support, genuine affection
and profound faith in us. As we journey together, we can’t but extend our deepest
thankfulness to God Almighty for blessing us with each of you, precious alumni.

When I took charge as the Principal of St Josephs’ Boys’ High School in June 2025, it was but
natural to have some level of trepidation. Having to fill the big shoes of my predecessor, Fr
Sunil Fernandes, I did have apprehensions of my new role regarding strengthening liaisons
with various stakeholders. Conversely, I must admit with ardent appreciation the patronage
and collaboration extended to me by the OBA, has been truly incredible!

Like the years in the recent past, this academic year too was studded with activities for our
students’ holistic development. The OBA Buzz, Out-Reach Programmes, Positive Parenting
Sessions, Annual Athletic Meet, SJBHS MUN, Cadence, Dynamix, Phenomenon, Infinity, Meraki,
Bifrost, Transcendence, Class Picnics and Excursions, Wellness Sessions, Creative Writing
Opportunities and the myriad Celebrations for various special occasions speak volumes of the
productive and exciting world of a typical Josephite. We can provide our students with these
abundant prospects, to shine brightly while lighting the way for others as well, solely due to
the benevolent presence and involvement of the OBA.

We are forever grateful to the OBA for their ongoing support to the Management, Staff and
Students. We would like to express our heartfelt appreciation to the OBA President, Mr. Sunil
Rasquinha, the Secretary, Mr. Roger Doss, and the Committee Members for their dedicated
efforts to make the Old Boys’ Association a pivotal part of the school.

We wish the OBA remarkable success as it strives continually to reach greater heights with
wider visions.
May God bless each one of us.

Fr Norwin Pereira, SJ
Principal

Message from The President
My Fellow Josephites,

Greetings to you all! It’s been an eventful year since I took over as
President. Also, a rewarding experience with so many wins and hits to
report.

We experienced some amazing activity & fellowship over the OBA
weekend, the Football, Golf & Cricket Tournaments, the Chennai, Coorg
& UK connects, the Christmas Dinner, Welcome to the OBA and The
Josephite Run 2026. It’s been heartwarming to see the levels of
participation in these events – both to organise and partake in them.
I’m grateful to one and all who did.
On the administration front we have taken many strides forward in ironing out processes for
donations from Old Boys around the world, while highlighting the causes & impact the OBA
makes in School. I’m grateful to the support from the BJES & JAANA on this account.
The BJES has also been very supportive in facilitating the new OBA Pavilion despite the
numerous constraints that need to be overcome.

Our funds, with efficient management, have grown further, thereby allowing us to enhance our
support to School. We are in the process of reorganizing some of the schemes in line with the
school’s needs and donor intentions.
Our flagship program - The Teachers Insurance Scheme - has been renewed and is further
strengthened by the fund raising from the ‘Josephite Run’. We will share a detailed update on
OBA causes and the support we have received in Episode 2 of this Calling.

We are improving our communication further and upgrading our website and forums. Kindly
update your contact & email details with the links provided to keep in touch with the OBA and
access your fellow Old Boys.

As we head towards end of the financial year, I urge you all to be generous and support our
many causes. I am very grateful to all those who have contributed so generously so far this year.

I look forward to the year ahead and hope to engage more with you around the next set of
initiatives we take up.

Fide et Labore

Sunil Rasquinha

The Year So Far
OBA DAY 2025
OBA Day of St. Joseph’s Boys’ High School was celebrated with great enthusiasm, dignity, and
a strong sense of fraternity, making it a truly memorable occasion for the Old Boys’
community. The event was meticulously planned and seamlessly executed, reflecting the
commitment and coordination of the organising committee and volunteers.

The celebrations commenced with Holy Mass, setting a solemn and meaningful tone for the
day. The wreath procession and flag hoisting ceremony were conducted with precision and
pride, honouring and remembering Old Boys who have laid down their lives for the country.
The active participation of Old Boys from different batches added further significance to the
occasion. Old Boys assembled house-wise to unfurl the School Flag and sing the School
Song, reinforcing a deep sense of unity and tradition.

The Annual General Meeting formed the formal core of OBA Day and was conducted in an
orderly and transparent manner. Important deliberations, presentations, and recognitions
were carried out smoothly, reinforcing the Association’s strong governance and shared
vision. The felicitation of distinguished Old Boys and the appreciation of staff members were
particularly well received.

Fellowship remained central throughout the day, with shared meals, informal interactions,
and sporting activities such as the hockey exhibition match fostering camaraderie and
reconnecting old friendships. Overall, OBA Day was a resounding success, strengthening
bonds among alumni and reaffirming their enduring connection to St. Joseph’s Boys’ High
School and its values.

Annual General Meeting 2025
The Annual General Meeting (AGM) of the Old Boys’ Association was conducted with
commendable order, professionalism, and active participation, reflecting the Association’s
strong commitment to transparency, inclusivity, and effective governance. With over 300
Old Boys and Old Girls in attendance across batches, the AGM underscored the strength
and unity of the Josephite alumni community.

The proceedings commenced on a solemn and reflective note with a prayer and a minute’s
silence in remembrance of Old Boys who had passed away during the year. This was
followed by the presentation of Lifetime and Significant Achievement Awards, recognising
outstanding Old Boys for their contributions and accomplishments. The President’s address
outlined key initiatives, milestones, and the Association’s vision, while the Principal’s address
reaffirmed the enduring bond between the school and its alumni. Members subsequently
confirmed the minutes of the previous AGM, ensuring continuity and accountability.

Key statutory and administrative matters were then taken up in a structured manner,
including the presentation and adoption of the Annual Report and Statement of Accounts,
approval of the auditor for the forthcoming term, and the discussion and adoption of
proposed resolutions. The election of the Managing Committee and office bearers for the
2025–26 term was conducted smoothly, reflecting strong member confidence in the
leadership.

The meeting concluded with a formal vote of thanks and transitioned into the traditional
spirit of Josephite camaraderie over lunch, marking a successful AGM that reinforced unity,
purpose, and the continued growth of the OBA.

Delhi Connect 2026
The SJBHS OBA Delhi Connect 2026 was held on 21st and 22nd February 2026 in New Delhi,
bringing together Josephites and their families for a memorable two-day reunion in the
capital city.

The Connect was organised by Sanjeev Mahajan (’75), Guru Kohli (’78), Col. Maini (’73), and
Anuj Dayal (’77), with representation from batches spanning 1962 to 2022. The gathering
was graced by Fr. Norwin Pereira SJ, Principal of SJBHS, along with three Office Bearers of
the OBA Managing Committee.

The programme commenced on Saturday with dinner at the famous Chicken Inn, Pandara
Road. Sunday began with a visit to the Delhi Metro Museum, followed by the Connect
luncheon get-together at AEPTA Army Golf Course, Delhi Cantonment. Fr. Norwin Pereira SJ
and OBA President Sunil Rasquinha addressed the gathering. Fr. Norwin acknowledged the
role of the OBA and warmly welcomed all Old Boys to the School, while Sunil Rasquinha
shared details of the major initiatives undertaken by the OBA. Both thanked the organisers
and attendees for making the Connect meaningful and enjoyable.

A moment’s silence was observed in memory of all deceased Old Boys.

The customary group photograph was followed by the School Song, led by the Batch of
2022. The Connect concluded with a sumptuous lunch.

Josephite Run 2026
What began just two years ago as a modest initiative has now grown into a landmark annual
tradition. In only its third edition, the Josephite Run witnessed an extraordinary turnout of
over 3,000 enthusiastic participants. Runners, students, families, alumni and well-wishers
came together in the true Josephite spirit of service. The grounds echoed with energy,
nostalgia, and shared purpose.

On the 18th of January 2026, the third edition of our signature charity run returned home
to the grounds of St. Joseph’s Boys High School. What made this year truly special was not
just the scale, but the sentiment. The ceremonial flag-off of the Josephite Run 2026 was
conducted from the SJBHS campus by Rector Fr. Daniel Fernandes, Principal Fr. Norwin
Pereira, Primary School Administrator Fr. Paul Anand, Vice Principal Brian Mckertish, Sunil
Rasquinha – SJBHS OBA President and Ambrish Sinha – Unext CEO. A new route was
charted, but the starting line was the place that shaped us all. It felt less like an event and
more like a homecoming.

This year’s edition marked tremendous growth in both scale and impact. The run raised vital
funds to support quality education for underprivileged students and to contribute to the
welfare of our beloved teachers at SJBHS. Every step taken that January morning carried
meaning. Every stride reflected gratitude. Every participant became part of something larger
than themselves.

As we look ahead, the Josephite Run stands not just as an annual event, but as a symbol of
who we are and what we believe in.

Women Who Lead - Talk Initiative by SJBHS OBA
The OBA Managing Committee has launched a new series titled 'Women Who Lead'.

Through this initiative, we invite accomplished women from diverse professional fields to
share their career journeys with our students. The aim is to create a space where our Grade
12 girls can hear real experiences, explore different career paths and gain practical insights
beyond textbooks.

Each session is designed to be interactive, allowing students to ask questions, engage in
meaningful conversations, and learn directly from women leading in their respective
industries. We hope this initiative empowers our girl students at SJBHS and inspires
confidence, ambition and clarity as they prepare for the next phase of their lives.

The first session featured Ms. Jayna Kothari. She offered the students a meaningful
introduction to the world of law and the realities of working within the Indian legal system.

She spoke candidly about her journey, sharing personal experiences, challenges, and the
values that have guided her career and life. The students were thoroughly engaged, and the
highlight of the session was the interactive Q&A segment, during which many asked
thoughtful and relevant questions.

The session proved both informative and empowering, setting a strong tone for future
'Women Who Lead' talks
