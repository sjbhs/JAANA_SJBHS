About This Edition
The OBA Calling is more than a newsletter. It is a structured communication initiative
designed to strengthen the bond between SJBHS OBA and its members across the globe.

Part 2 of the OBA Calling Trilogy focuses on Giving with Purpose — donations, the causes
supported by the OBA, contributions received and their impact, mechanisms to donate,
and the governance processes that ensure accountability. It aims to provide clarity,
transparency, and confidence to all who wish to give back.

Over the years, the OBA has been able to lead and support many an initiative for School —
Infrastructure, Scholarships, Mid-Day Meals, Awards, and Teacher's Insurance. These have
been supported by Old Boys through Batch funds, Individual funds, and direct
contributions. Batch and Individual funds are reported in our Annual Report every year.
The OBA now aims to create a formal record of these contributions and periodically
publish them, starting with the most recent ones.

If any contribution has been missed, we urge you to bring it to the attention of the
Secretary and it will be included in the records.

Message from The President
Dear Fellow Josephites,

Over the years, The OBA has been able to lead / support many an
initiative for School. Infrastructure, Scholarships, Mid-Day Meals, Awards,
Teacher’s Insurance, Awards etc.. These have been supported by Old
Boys through Batch funds, Individual funds, direct contributions to
projects via the OBA, directly to School or through the Bangalore Jesuit
Education Society (BJES).

Batch & Individual funds are reported in the our Annual Report every
year. While some direct contributions by Old Boys are recorded by way
of plaques, reports etc, many have not been formally recorded.The OBA,
School; BJES are very grateful to every donor over the years for their generosity and support.
The OBA now aims to create a formal record of these contributions and periodically publish
them, starting with the most recent ones. If any contribution has been missed out, I urge you to
bring it to the attention of the Secretary and it will be included in the records.

The School has grown tremendously over the years and continues to progress. Despite this, it
continues to offer the same level of support to the underprivileged and financially weaker
sections of society. With this, the need for support too has grown. The OBA, to keep time with
this, must continuously raise the levels of support to School, financially and by way of time
invested.

Episode 2 of the Calling aims to highlight the areas of support, the need and contributions
received over the recent few years post covid-19, along with an overview of the OBA funds and
impact created. It also highlights the areas where the School needs support in terms of time,
money or knowhow.

We have many distinguished members who have scaled great heights, made a difference to
society or been passionate Josephites. They are an inspiration to all of us. Episode 3 of the
Calling will highlight a few of them.

All Old Boys & Girls are welcome to visit School, the OBA office, experience the progress made
and participate in this journey of shaping the future of our Society.

Fide et Labore

Sunil Rasquinha
President - SJBHS OBA

Giving with Your Time
At the OBA, we believe that giving back is not limited to financial contributions. The time,
expertise and passion of our alumni are equally valuable to the School and its students.

COACH AND TRAIN OUR STUDENTS

If you have excelled in a sport: cricket, football, golf, hockey, basketball, swimming or
athletics, we invite you to step onto the grounds as a mentor. Whether refining technique,
building mental fortitude or passing on the love of the game, your involvement makes a
lasting difference.

THE OBA BUZZ

OBA Buzz, the flagship and marquee event of the Old Boys’ Association, is a vibrant platform
conducted for students. Old Boys and Girls are warmly invited to contribute their time and
expertise by mentoring and guiding students or serving as judges across cultural events
such as dramatics, quiz, dumb charades and debate. Your involvement, even for a day, can
significantly enhance the experience for students and strengthen the spirit of the OBA
community.

ACADEMIC AND PROFESSIONAL MENTORSHIP

Many students benefit greatly from one-on-one or group mentorship. If you have expertise
in any domain — competitive examinations, entrepreneurship, technology, the sciences or
the humanities — volunteering a few hours can open doors for a young student that they
never imagined possible.

CREATIVE AND CULTURAL CONTRIBUTIONS

Alumni with skills in music, theatre, visual arts, public speaking or writing are encouraged to
conduct workshops, adjudicate competitions, or simply engage with students who share
those passions.

HOW TO VOLUNTEER

Old Boys and Girls who wish to give back through time and expertise may reach out to the
OBA Secretary.

Write to us: secretary@sjbhsoba.net

Batch of 1977 Social Responsibility Project
The St. Joseph’s Boys’ High School (SJBHS) Batch of 1977, fondly known as the “77ers,”
recently marked the second anniversary of its partnership to develop a holistic education
program at St. Joseph School, Pandithahalli (SJS-PH), an SJBHS-affiliated school located in the
rural Mandya district of Karnataka.

SJS-PH is dedicated to uplifting under-privileged children through secular English-medium
education, with a focus on forming students of conscience, competence, compassion, and
commitment. Each school day begins with a non-denominational prayer, and students
celebrate Diwali, Ramzan, and Christmas together, reflecting the school’s inclusive ethos. In
a proud connection to their alma mater, SJS-PH students also wear the same house colors
as those at St. Joseph’s Boys’ High School, Bengaluru.

Since 2020, SJS-PH has grown significantly—from 150 to 750 students—serving
predominantly children from Scheduled Caste and Tribe (SC/ST) and Other Backward Class
(OBC) communities. As the school expands access to quality education across remote rural
areas, the 77ers have partnered closely to upgrade key infrastructure, including the library,
sports facilities, staff room, classrooms, and science laboratories. The introduction of
Lenovo tablets has further strengthened digital and online learning. In addition, the
partnership has launched several student-focused initiatives, including martial arts
programs, an annual sports day, health camps, learning disability awareness programs, and
teacher training sessions.

The Social Responsibility Project began with the creation of a well-resourced library,
grounded in the belief that strong reading and communication skills are essential for
academic success and personal growth. Alumni members donated books and continue to
sponsor and actively support the library, helping students build comprehension skills and
confidence in communication.

To foster self-confidence and discipline, the 77ers introduced structured sports programs
and individual competitions, beginning with a martial arts initiative led by a national-level
coach from the alumni group. Students from Pandithahalli have since excelled at local,
regional, and state-level competitions, demonstrating remarkable growth in confidence and
focus. The partnership also revived a traditional annual sports day, modeled on the century-
old sports culture of SJBHS, with alumni families sponsoring championship shields and
prizes.

Academic excellence remains a core priority. The 77ers strengthened science education
through the establishment of the Peter Pinto Science Laboratory and further advanced
STEM learning with the creation of the Loyola Technology Center. Equipped with Lenovo
Smart pads and hands-on technology resources, the center enables students to connect
classroom theory with practical experimentation and participate in inter-school STEM
competitions.

Every successful partnership is driven by committed leadership. SJS-PH’s Fr. Pradeep
(Correspondent) and Fr. Alwyn (Principal), along with SJBHS 77ers, Sunil Dalwani, Prapanch
Mandana, Glenn Rego, Sanjay Sahani and Thomas Thekkethala, have played a pivotal role in
transforming shared ideas and aspirations into reality. Rooted in Jesuit values of service,
“faith and toil,” this partnership seeks to replicate the best practices of our alma mater at
SJS-PH while inspiring other alumni groups to pay forward their education and uplift
students in rural and disadvantaged communities across Karnataka.

We invite fellow 77ers and alumni to join us on this meaningful and deeply rewarding
journey.
